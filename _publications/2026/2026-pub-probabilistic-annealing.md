abstract: >-
  Large-scale sparse multi-objective optimization problems (LSMOPs) are prevalent in real-world applications, where optimal solutions typically contain only a few nonzero variables. The coexistence of sparsity and high dimensionality greatly intensifies the conflict between exploration and exploitation. This paper proposes an evolutionary algorithm with probabilistic annealing driven by two probability vectors with distinct entropy characteristics: a convergence-oriented probability vector ensures stable exploitation, whereas an annealed probability vector enables an adaptive transition from global exploration to local refinement.
